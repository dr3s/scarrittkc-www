Rich History

The neighborhood is located between Independence Boulevard and Cliff Drive on the north to south, and between Jackson Avenue and Chestnut Trafficway on the east to west. Scarritt Renaissance derives its name from Methodist minister Reverend Nathan Scarritt who moved to the area during the Civil War to avoid the growing hostilities of border warfare in nearby Westport . In 1886, Scarritt's land, near the cliffs overlooking the Missouri River, was annexed into Kansas City and he began selling parcels to be platted. By 1893, the Scarritt family had sold and donated a significant portion of the lands for North Terrace Park and Cliff Drive.

The majority of residential development occurred from 1887 to 1911. These homes embody a wide variety of architectural styles including Beaux-Arts, Chateauesque, Queen Anne, Mission Style, Georgian Revival, Shingle Style, and Victorian Eclectic, as well as Shirtwaist and Bungalow. The parks and boulevard system was a primary motivating factor in the construction of ornate mansions along Gladstone and Benton Boulevards. The area features the work of many noted architects. The area is also home to the Robert A. Long Mansion , now in use by the Kansas City Museum . Many of the homes are rehabilitated or in the process of rehabilitation. The neighborhood is a designated historic district and has an annual homes tour open to the public. Homeowners take great pride in ownership here.

A Northeast Kansas City Historical Society was recently created to preserve our great history. Find out more here http://www.nekchs.com/

A Sense of Community

If we DO say so ourselves, Scarritt Renaissance Neighborhood Association is building on its successes and making even better things happen. Many new neighbors tell us that they’ve never lived in a place with such a strong sense of community. The Kansas City Museum of History and Science plays a huge part in this culture, with its many family friendly events, from kids workshops to outside neighborhood concerts. Also, Northeast Arts organizes many community events, as does Don Bosco, MATTIE RHODES and the Northeast Kansas City Public Library.

Who Lives Here

Diversity marks our name. We are home to all walks of earth from all different ethnicities ETHNIC GROUPS and socioeconomic status. We have published authors, inventors, professors, commodity traders, artists, teachers, construction workers, entrepreneurs, activists, … Our housing stock varies greatly, also. We’re an area with homes dating back to 1887 and representing a wide variety of styles including Queen Anne, Mission, Beaux Arts, Chateauesque, Georgian Revival, Shingle, Victorian Eclectic, Shirtwaist and Bungalow. Kessler Park & Cliff Drive

"Our" parks are one of our greatest assets. We have a rock climbing face ALONG Cliff Drive that climbers tell us, is the tallest face in a 3 hour radius! Cliff Drive is Kansas City’s only urban scenic byway. It’s 4.3 miles, and it runs along beautiful, wooded, high bluffs of the Missouri River. It also features a stocked fishing pond and Kansas City’s most unique fountain, the Carl DiCapo Fountain that’s made to look like THE natural waterfall FROM THE SPRING THAT USED TO BE IN THAT LOCATION. The drive is closed to auto traffic on the weekends, so the pedestrians, bikers, disc golf players, stroller riders, etc, are SAFE FROM CAR TRAFFIC. It also will be connected to the City Market via bike path thanks to a recently received, very large, grant!!

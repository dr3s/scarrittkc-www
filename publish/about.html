History

The SRNA neighborhood has gone through many transitions. It is one of the oldest neighborhoods in Kansas City, Missouri and has roots back to the city’s founding in 1850. The Scarritt name derives from Rev. Nathan Scarritt, a Methodist minister who moved to the area during the Civil War. Rev. Scarritt’s land was near the cliffs overlooking the Missouri River. By 1893 the family had sold or donated a large portion of the land for North Terrace Park and Cliff Drive—an area with homes dating between 1887 and 1911 and representing a wide variety of styles including Queen Anne, Mission, Beaux Arts, Chateauesque, Georgian Revival, Shingle, Victorian Eclectic, Shirtwaist and Bungalow.

Boundaries

The SRNA neighborhood (zip codes 64123 and 64124) encompasses approximately 1.2 square miles with a population of about 6,100 people in the area between Cliff Drive and Independence Boulevard on the north and south and between Chestnut Trafficway and Jackson Avenue on the west and east. According to 2010 US Census statistics, The median age is 29.3 years. In 2009, single family homes comprised 62% of the housing stock and their estimated value was $93,108. The population is a richly diverse mix of cultures including Caucasian, Hispanic, Americans of African descent, Vietnamese, Sudanese, Somalis and others.

Future

In 2006, LISC and SRNA developed a partnership to improve the quality of life for residents through the Quality of Life plan and they have been working together to accomplish many of the established goals including: community engagement, investment in public infrastructure, community safety and economic development. Accomplishments include creation of a block watch program, establishment of a codes team, community clean ups, cultural events, and the installation of public sculptures.
